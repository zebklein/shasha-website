About Me


  I'm a Michigan licensed psychologist with a part-time private practice in northeast Ann Arbor. I received a Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology from Michigan State University (1995) and a Post-Doctoral Fellowship in Professional Psychology (Unconscious Processes), Department of Psychiatry, University of Michigan (1998). My approach to psychotherapy emphasizes the therapeutic relationship over specific techniques. I view each individual as unique and collaborate to develop a shared understanding of their treatment needs.
  My office is located in a professional building with ample parking. Confidential video sessions are available.

General goals of psychodynamic therapy within a Christian worldview


Greater awareness and tolerance of your interior life of thoughts, feelings, memories, dreams and hopes.

Better ability to regulate your emotions, impulses, and conflicts using reason and wisdom.

The therapeutic relationship is used as a 'tool ' for growth and resilience.

Greater acceptance of the limitations of others and therefore more realistic relationships.

Freedom to pursue God and have a more realistic view of Him as a loving Father.

Greater reliance and intimacy with God in a direct relationship.
